## General Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is Standard Money?">
    <p>Standard Money is a synthetic dollar protocol that makes USDT what it was always meant to be. We offer USDsd, a synthetic, collateralized version of Tether that provides a stable and scalable solution to the trader market with up to 30% annual yield potential.</p>
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is USDsd?">
  <p>
    USDsd is a synthetic, collateralized version of Tether that provides a
    stable and scalable solution to the trader market. Unlike traditional
    stablecoins, USDsd offers up to 30% annual yield through advanced basis
    trading strategies.
  </p>
</Accordion>

{" "}

<Accordion title="What is sUSDsd?">
  <p>
    sUSDsd (staked USDsd) is the yield-bearing version of USDsd. When you stake
    USDsd, you receive sUSDsd tokens that automatically compound rewards from
    protocol revenue, currently earning 8% APY with potential for up to 30%.
  </p>
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does USDsd maintain its peg?">
    <p>USDsd maintains its peg through being 100% collateralized by USDT combined with sophisticated basis trading strategies. The protocol uses advanced algorithms to short perps and long spot simultaneously, earning yields from exchanges like Binance, OKX, and Bybit.</p>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Minting & Staking

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="How do I stake Usdsd?">
  <p>To stake Usdsd:</p>
  <ol>
    <li>Go to the staking section in the Standard Money app</li>
    <li>Enter the amount of Usdsd you want to stake</li>
    <li>Confirm the transaction</li>
    <li>You'll receive sUsdsd tokens that represent your staked position</li>
  </ol>
</Accordion>

{" "}

<Accordion title="How do sUsdsd rewards work?">
  <p>
    sUsdsd rewards are automatically compounded into your sUsdsd balance. The
    rewards come from:
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Funding payments from perpetual futures positions</li>
    <li>Protocol revenue distribution</li>
  </ul>
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I unstake sUsdsd?">
    <p>Yes, you can unstake sUsdsd at any time to receive Usdsd back. The unstaking process may have a cooldown period depending on protocol parameters.</p>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Risks & Security

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What are the main risks of using Usdsd?">
    <p>The main risks include:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Funding Risk:</strong> Negative funding periods can impact protocol revenue</li>
      <li><strong>Liquidation Risk:</strong> Hedging positions may face liquidation in extreme conditions</li>
      <li><strong>Custodial Risk:</strong> Risk of custodian failure or compromise</li>
      <li><strong>Smart Contract Risk:</strong> Risk of bugs or exploits in smart contracts</li>
      <li><strong>Exchange Risk:</strong> Risk of exchange failure or manipulation</li>
    </ul>
  </Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if the protocol is hacked?">
  <p>
    In the unlikely event of a hack, the protocol has emergency procedures in
    place including pause mechanisms and governance processes to address the
    situation. The reserve fund may also be used to cover losses.
  </p>
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I verify I'm using the correct contracts?">
    <p>Always verify contract addresses on the official <a href="/solution-design/key-addresses">Key Addresses</a> page and cross-reference with Bscscan. Never use contracts from unofficial sources.</p>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
